What the evidence establishes — and what it does not
This is not a disclaimer. It is the most important part of the analysis. Knowing the limits of the evidence is what separates statistical witness from advocacy.

The null hypothesis — that civilian casualties resulted from random collateral damage in compliance with international humanitarian law's proportionality requirements — is rejected at p < 0.0001. This is the finding. It is not the same as any of the things in the right-hand column above.
